How to respond

Our response to a source can take many forms, including the following:

  • personal response (our views and experiences);
  • exploratory and interpretive response (exploring what we think the source means); and
  • critical response (not necessarily negative--but raising questions about the value, purpose, significance, and context of the source).

You can base a response on many different kinds of questions--but that is what a response fundamentally does: answers a question you or someone else has posed.

  • How do I relate to this source?
  • What experiences have I had that are similar or different?
  • What does the author/ speaker mean when she says ____?
  • How would someone very different from the author/ speaker understand what the source text is describing?
  • Why is this source--regardless of when it was composed--relevant now, today: how does it speak to our contemporary moment?
  • What is the most important idea in this source, and why is it important socially, culturally, politically, or in our local (geographic) context?
